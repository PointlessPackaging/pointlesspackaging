MISSION

 

Large online retailers ship millions of packages each day, and often these products are shipped in packaging that is both wasteful and harmful. The packaging is often excessive and containing nonbiodegradable materials such as plastic. The environment pays the price, our oceans, air quality, and environment are all impacted. The aim of this project is to draw attention to these practices. We created a system, which uses images to automatically detect oversized shipping boxes and harmful materials. Our method uses computer vision techniques to analyze the images and calculate a packaging grade. With the results our algorithm returned we could present the companies who used the most efficient packaging, informing people who the most efficient packagers are. Hopefully, this can lead to packaging practices being changed for the better.

Pointless packaging is a project developed by engineering students at UC Davis. We chose this project to explore some of the interesting subjects of computer science such as computer vision and machine learning, while also having a positive impact.
